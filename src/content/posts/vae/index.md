A **Variational Autoencoder** is a type of generative model designed to model complex data distributions $\large p(x)$ using latent variables z:

- **Encoder:** Maps input x to a latent distribution $\large q_\phi(z|x)$ (probabilistic).  
- **Decoder:** Maps \(z\) back to data space via $\large p_\theta(x|z)$.  

Key idea: Learn a **probabilistic mapping** to a latent space instead of a deterministic code.

---

## Probabilistic Model

Assume data is generated by latent variable z:

$$
\large p_\theta(x) = \int p_\theta(x, z) \, dz = \int p_\theta(x|z)p(z) \, dz
$$

- p(z): prior on latent variables (often $\large \mathcal{N}(0, I)$)  
- $\large p_\theta(x|z)$: likelihood (decoder)

**Goal:** Maximize log-likelihood:

$$
\large \log p_\theta(x) = \log \int p_\theta(x|z)p(z) \, dz
$$

Problem: The integral is usually **intractable** for high-dimensional data.

---

## Variational Inference

Introduce a variational distribution $\large q_\phi(z|x)$ to approximate the true posterior $\large p_\theta(z|x)$.  

Maximize the **Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO):**

$$
\large \log p_\theta(x) \geq \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)} [\log p_\theta(x|z)] - \text{KL}(q_\phi(z|x) \parallel p(z))
$$

- $\large \mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)} [\log p_\theta(x|z)]$ → **Reconstruction term**  
- $\large \text{KL}(q_\phi(z|x) \parallel p(z))$ → **Regularization term** (ensures smooth latent space)

---

## Choosing the Distributions

- **Encoder:** $\large q_\phi(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\phi(x), \text{diag}(\sigma^2_\phi(x)))$ 
- **Decoder:** $\large p_\theta(x|z) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta(z), \sigma^2 I)$ (continuous) or Bernoulli (binary)  
- **Prior:** $\large p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, I)$

---

##  KL Divergence (Closed Form)

For $\large q_\phi(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)$ and $p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$:

$$
\large \text{KL}(q_\phi(z|x) \parallel p(z)) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^d \left( \sigma_i^2 + \mu_i^2 - 1 - \log \sigma_i^2 \right)
$$

where d = latent dimension.

---

## Reparameterization Trick

To backpropagate through sampling $\large z \sim q_\phi(z|x)$:

$$
\large z = \mu_\phi(x) + \sigma_\phi(x) \odot \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)
$$

This makes z differentiable w.r.t. $\phi$ for gradient descent.

---

## Final Loss Function

For a single data point x:

$$
\large \mathcal{L}(\theta, \phi; x) = -\mathbb{E}_{z \sim q_\phi(z|x)}[\log p_\theta(x|z)] + \text{KL}(q_\phi(z|x) \parallel p(z))
$$

- Minimize this loss → maximize ELBO → approximate maximum likelihood.

---

### Summary

- **Encoder:** $\large x \mapsto q_\phi(z|x) = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)$ 
- **Sampling:** $\large z = \mu + \sigma \odot \epsilon$
- **Decoder:** $\large z \mapsto p_\theta(x|z)$
- **Loss:** Reconstruction + KL divergence  

VAEs combine **probabilistic modeling** with **deep learning** and produce a **smooth, interpretable latent space** for generative tasks.
